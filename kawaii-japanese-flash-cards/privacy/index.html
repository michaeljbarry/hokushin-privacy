Privacy Policy

Kawaii Japanese Flash Cards

Kawaii Japanese Flash Cards is a language-learning app for practicing Japanese vocabulary and study progress. This policy explains what information the app uses and how that information is handled.

Effective July 22, 2026 Applies to Kawaii Japanese Flash Cards

Short version: Kawaii Japanese Flash Cards is designed for private, offline study. The app does not require an account, collect or transmit your study data to the developer, include advertising, or track you.

Information the App Uses

The app stores information generated through normal use, including deck and display preferences, study progress, starred cards, review history, accuracy counts, daily goals, streaks, and any in-progress session checkpoint. The app does not provide account registration or free-form personal profile fields.

Storage

Study data and preferences are stored locally on your device so the app can preserve progress and settings. The app does not synchronize that data to a developer-operated server. If your device uses Apple system backup services, Apple may store backup copies according to your Apple account and device settings.

The app does not require you to create a separate account with the developer.

If you export a study-progress backup, the copy is saved only to the location you select through Apple's document interface. You control and are responsible for that exported file.

Analytics, Ads, and Tracking

The app contains no third-party analytics SDK, advertising SDK, or tracking technology. It does not sell personal data or send study activity to the developer.

If optional Apple diagnostics or crash sharing is enabled on your device, Apple may provide the developer with diagnostic information under Apple's privacy settings. This is controlled by Apple and your device settings, not by an in-app analytics service.

Purchases

The current app release includes no in-app purchases, subscriptions, or other paid feature unlocks.

Retention and Deletion

Study data remains on your device while the app is installed. Removing the app deletes its local container. Apple system backups may retain a copy until you update or delete those backups through your Apple account settings. A study-progress backup you exported remains wherever you saved it until you delete it there.

Children's Privacy

Kawaii Japanese Flash Cards is a language-learning app and is not designed to collect personal contact information from children. The app has no developer account system or personal profile submission feature. A parent or guardian with a privacy question can contact the developer below.

Security

The app relies on Apple platform protections and your device security settings to help protect locally stored data. No security method is perfect, so you should protect your device passcode and Apple ID.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated as the app changes. The effective date at the top of the page will be updated when material changes are made.

Contact

For privacy questions about Kawaii Japanese Flash Cards, contact the developer through the public GitHub profile at github.com/michaeljbarry.
